Equilibrium option pricing: A Monte Carlo approach



This website has as purpose the implementation of the paper developed by Axel Buchner that presents a novel Monte Carlo method for option pricing which is based on a general equilibrium model.

The main advantage of the method is that it does not require the specification of a market price of risk, making the method flexible for pricing in incomplete markets which is a well-known challenge in financial markets.

The method produces a strongly consistent estimator for the option price that exhibits the same error convergence rate as the standard risk-neutral pricing Monte Carlo approach.






In the Iterations option, we can check how different numbers of simulation iterations can affect the option prices given by the model.

As showed in the results, analyzing the standard errors, we find that the model has an O(√n) convergence rate. Also, we can see that the standard errors grow with increasing moneyness of the options.

ITERATIONS

In the Relative Risk Aversion option, we can understand how different levels of relative risk aversion b on option prices.

We concluded that, in general, increasing levels of risk aversion decreases call option prices and increase put option prices.In incomplete market structures, the relative risk aversion has an important effect on option prices.


RELATIVE RISK AVERSION

In the Volatility option, we compare how different types of volatility (constant and stochastic) can affect the option prices.

Overall, we concluded that a stochastic volatility generates increased prices of In-The-Money (ITM) call options and decreases the prices of Out-The-Money (OTM) call options. Having an the opposite effect in put options.
